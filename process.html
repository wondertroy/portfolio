First Steps: defining our goals

The project began as a challenge to create an interactive space that could be useful in the context of early learning.


We immediately started thinking of cooperative problem solving exercises, drawing inspiration from games such as portal 2.


Illustrated on the right is an early brainstorming concept using a kinect, however this was discarded as it did not use 3d space effectively and was essentially just an interactive surface.

Multi-Planar Maze

A refined concept for an interactive space that translates 3d area in to two 2d planes.

The initial thinking was that the planes would be projected on to the wall and floor of the play area.

3 April 2017

Rapid Prototyping in unity

Prototyping levels was farely simple, as they could easily be built using simple cubes.

Once objects were created, they could be saved as prefabs and reused later on which sped up the process of creating levels.

Scripting behavior

Using prefabs supplied with the steamVR plugin, it was very quick and easy to get the htc vive controllers tracking and interacting with the game.

Tags and public variables were used when scripting the cubes to follow the controllers. This allowed any modifications to be done within the unity editor without having to rewrite any code.

26 April 2017

User Testing

We needed to make sure that the space would not be too difficult to navigate

1 May 2017


Feedback

While I used VR to demonstrate the prototype, at this point I was still intending to have the planes projected on to the floor and wall and not use VR at all, however it quickly became apparent that using Virtual Reality would provide a more exciting experience.

The feedback that we got from the prototype indicated that it looked too difficult and frustrating to navigate.

For cooperative games it is important that the skill gap is minimised since it can cause negative experience between participants

One suggestion was to have separate environments for VR and physical reality that are linked together.

7 May 2017

Incremental Prototyping
