21 March 2017

Interactive Environments and Early learning

The project began as a challenge to create an interactive space that could be useful in the context of early learning.

We chose these themes because we believe that physical interaction can be a very effective delivery method for learning at all stages.

28 March 2017

First Steps: defining our goals

Drawing inspiration from games such as portal 2, we started thinking of cooperative problem solving exercises, that could make use of an interactive space


Illustrated on the right is an early brainstorming concept using a kinect, however this was discarded as it did not use 3d space effectively and was essentially just an interactive surface.

28 March 2017

Multi-Planar Maze

A refined concept for an interactive space that translates 3D area in to two 2D planes.

The initial thinking was that the planes would be projected on to the wall and floor of the play area.

3 April 2017

Rapid Prototyping in unity

Prototyping levels was farely simple, as they could easily be built using simple cubes.

Once objects were created, they could be saved as prefabs and reused later on which sped up the process of creating levels.

Scripting behavior

Using prefabs supplied with the steamVR plugin, it was very quick and easy to get the htc vive controllers tracking and interacting with the game.

Tags and public variables were used when scripting the cubes to follow the controllers. This allowed any modifications to be done within the unity editor without having to rewrite any code.

12 April 2017

Implementing Leo's Level Designs

While I was developing the core gameplay functionality in a simple placeholder level, Leo was designing levels that force cooperation and provide a sense of increasing challenge

I was able to quickly build levels based on Leo's documents using prefabs that I had already made and simply dragging and dropping them in to a new scene and repositioning them.

26 April 2017

User Testing

We needed to make sure that the space would not be too difficult to navigate

1 May 2017


Feedback

While I used VR to demonstrate the prototype, at this point I was still intending to have the planes projected on to the floor and wall and not use VR at all, however it quickly became apparent that using Virtual Reality would provide a more exciting experience.

The feedback that we got from the prototype indicated that it looked too difficult and frustrating to navigate.

For cooperative games it is important that the skill gap is minimised since it can cause negative experience between participants

One suggestion was to have separate environments for VR and physical reality that are linked together.

7 May 2017

Incremental Prototyping

The revised concept was easy to test since the level in unity only had to be slightly modified.

24 May 2017

Incorporating Daya's Assets

Up to this point all the models in the game were simple cubes or planes. While I was working on gameplay functionality, Daya was working on 3d models to improve the visual style and give the game a theme.

Replacing the placeholder models was a farely simple task, as it was ntively supported in the unity editor. All that was required was some resizing.

I ended up changing the VR environment to be inside the maze as it was more interesting than looking down on it.

30 May 2017

Refining for the Exhibit

We had to simplify the challenge quite a lot for the exhibit to ensure that guests could quickly get an idea of what the project is all about. The goal was to have participants in and out in 15 minutes.

Further user testing revealed that the corridors were too narrow and the cubes would accidentally hit the walls too easily which frustrated the participants. A simple modification of the level fixed these issues.

Reflection

While the prototype for the exhibit was a good experience, there were a few components missing or that needed tweaking.

The pressure pads needed integration testing far earlier in the project to ensure that they would work and could easily be set up.

The game needed a reset button specifically for the exhibit to streamline the process of getting many guests through the experience in a short amount of time.

There could have been more user testing on the difficulty of the level before the exhibit. This could have avoided some negative tensions that were apparent for some teams that participated.
